Geothermal basics

Geothermal is a heat pump, with a better energy source.

A geothermal heat pump works on the same basic principle as other heat pumps. The difference is the source of energy.

Air-source heat pumps use outdoor air as the energy source. Geothermal and water-source systems use the more stable temperature of the ground, water, or building loop. That stable source can help deliver reliable heating and cooling performance, even when outdoor temperatures change.

For the right home, building, or project, geothermal can provide excellent long-term comfort, efficiency, and operating cost advantages.

Look beyond upfront cost

Compare systems honestly.

Air-source heat pumps can be a practical option for many homes. Geothermal systems usually cost more upfront, but they may offer stronger long-term operating savings, especially for larger homes, custom builds, commercial buildings, and projects where long-term efficiency matters.

We help clients compare options honestly so they can choose the system that makes the most sense for their property, budget, and long-term plans.
